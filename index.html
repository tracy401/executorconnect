The problem

You drafted the will. But did you know they died?

Most clients don't even make their families aware of existing estate planning documents, much less tell them about the attorney who drafted them. After a client dies, how can you encourage the family to work with you if you don't know about the death?

"Even though I try to scan the newspaper obituaries, I can't remember thirty years' worth of client names! And clients who've moved might not even have their obituaries published in our local paper."

— Estate planning attorney, Fort Wayne, Indiana
How it works

Simple setup. Automatic from there.

01

Upload a list of client names

Securely upload a list of client names and optional dates of birth. No software required — a simple spreadsheet upload or manual entry works fine.

02

Select cities and counties

Enter the counties or cities that are likely to be mentioned in your clients' obituaries. We'll review the full text of every national obituary using your search criteria, no matter where the obituaries are published.

03

Get alerted immediately

When a match is found, your firm receives an email alert the same day with the obituary link and the date of death — before families start calling other law firms.

04

Review and act

Confirm the match in your own records, then reach out to the family with confidence and care — at exactly the right moment. ExecutorConnect never contacts your clients or their family members.
